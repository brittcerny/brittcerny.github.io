About the Project

The Urban Cookie Forest is a community-driven urban forestry project focused on planting trees where they matter most. The project combines GIS mapping, field data collection, and public engagement to identify priority planting areas and track impact over time.

The Urban Cookie Forest is supported by partners whose missions align with sustainability, community engagement, and long-term impact. The Girl Scouts of the USA (GSUSA) empower girls to build courage, confidence, and character while promoting environmental stewardship through initiatives like the Tree Promise, which aims to plant 5 million trees by 2026. Their focus on leadership, inclusivity, and climate action makes them a natural partner in expanding urban tree planting efforts. Complementing this, the Better Business Bureau (BBB) fosters marketplace trust by promoting ethical business practices, supporting responsible organizations, and recognizing community-minded leadership. Together, these partners strengthen the project’s ability to connect environmental action with community and business engagement.
